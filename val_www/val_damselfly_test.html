River Jewelwing (Calopteryx aequabilis) Say 1839

Suggested Citation:

Pfeiffer, Bryan, Michael Blust, and Kent McFarland. 2018. Vermont Odonata Atlas. Vermont Center for Ecostudies – Vermont Atlas of Life. Retrieved from http://val.vtecostudies.org. DATE ACCESSED.

Relatively widely distributed in low- to mid-elevation streams and rivers around the state. Usually found in slower reaches where substrate is sandy to mucky and shoreline plants offer perching spots. Can be fairly common on some rivers, particularly where they empty into Lake Champlain or the Connecticut River. The lower Poultney River is one of the best sites in the state to find this insect.

Conservation Status

Vermont: S5
Global: S5

Flight Period

April 14 – October 22
